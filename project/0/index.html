Approaching this project was, honestly, kind of intimidating. While I'm familiar with design thinking and the design process, notions of a mind map and visualizations of key concepts aren't something I'm particularly used to nor am I fond of using in my own process, so completing this project was equally challenging and eye-opening to the fact that I honestly really enjoyed it.

It was sort of enlightening to see how my definitions of experience design are similar to what we discussed in class - imagery and color coincide with the second dimension, visuals; environment and sound coincide with the third dimension, objects; and people maybe coincides with the fourth or fifth dimensions, time and behavior, depending on the purpose of people in the experience I curate.

Through this, the project made me think about intention in my design â€“ how do I use all of these things to create an experience within my own definitions? Imagery and color are used to incite emotion, to provide a sense of familiarity in an otherwise strange situation, like an escape room; environment and sound do the same, setting the scene for new interactions. But people exist to guide, to add to the environment, and, well, to experience whatever I do design, so it was really interesting to see that humans exist at the core of my design process.
